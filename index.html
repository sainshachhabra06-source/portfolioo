Influencer Marketing

Scaling creator campaigns from intern task to owned process

Blubox Media · Oct 2025 – Mar 2026

The Brief

Blubox Media needed creator partnerships sourced, negotiated, and delivered on brand and on time, across multiple client campaigns running in parallel and spanning creator markets across North, South, East, and West India.

What I Did

Took ownership of end-to-end influencer campaigns: shortlisted creators, led outreach and rate negotiations, and managed delivery so content stayed on-brief and on schedule.

My creator vetting checklist

Before any creator was onboarded, across creator markets in North, South, East, and West India, I screened against eight checks, then matched the profile against the specific campaign objective rather than going on follower count alone:

01 / Audience relevance
02 / Follower count
03 / Engagement rate
04 / Average reel views
05 / Audience demographics
06 / Content quality
07 / Past brand collaborations
08 / Authenticity of engagement

For creators on tight turnarounds, I also stepped in past pure coordination, editing raw footage myself and briefing creators on angles and lighting before a shoot, so a delay on their end didn’t become a delay on the campaign.

Intern → Executive in 6 months 8-point creator vetting process Creators across N/S/E/W India Hands-on raw footage editing
Social Media: Startup Turnaround

Rebuilding a dead corporate page across 30 gym branches

WTF Gyms (startup) · Social Media Intern

The Brief

WTF Gyms’ main corporate Instagram page had gone inactive, and the account itself was carrying a heavy load of fake followers, making organic reach genuinely difficult to earn. Someone needed to rebuild the page from near-zero and bring consistency to 30 separate branch accounts.

What I Did

Initiated and rebuilt the corporate page from scratch, curating fitness and gym-focused content to re-engage a real audience. Handled scripting, storyboarding, shoot assistance, and editing end-to-end, while managing daily posting and content calendars across all 30 branch pages.

The honest part

The fake-follower problem on the account meant standard engagement benchmarks were misleading and reach was harder to earn than the follower count suggested. Rather than chase vanity metrics, I focused on consistent, fitness-relevant content and pushed for organic reach wherever the algorithm would allow it: the realistic ceiling on a compromised account, not a manufactured win.

30 branch pages, daily posting Corporate page rebuilt from dead Script → storyboard → shoot → edit Meta Business Suite scheduling
